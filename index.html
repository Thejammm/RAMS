Section 5: Emergency Arrangements

5.1 First Aid

The Site First Aider will be Site Supervisor ‚Äì Vincent Collingwood 073614 430571. First aid provisions will be made available both by the PCL site teams and the Principal Contractor and communicated upon the site induction. At this time any specialist first aid requirements required for PCL operatives the PC will be notified.

5.2 Rescue planning

This rescue plan sets out the procedures to follow in the event of an emergency during groundworks or excavation operations, including ground collapse, entrapment, service strikes, or persons or plant falling into an excavation. Its purpose is to ensure a safe, coordinated response that protects workers and enables quick medical assistance.
All site personnel must be familiar with these procedures. In the event of an incident, the site manager or supervisor will take control, ensure the area is made safe, and contact the emergency services. The appointed person will meet the emergency responders at the site entrance and direct them to the incident. Operatives must not attempt any rescue that would put themselves or others at risk.
If a collapse or entrapment occurs, work must stop immediately, the area must be evacuated, and all plant and machinery in the vicinity shut down. No one should re-enter the excavation until its stability has been confirmed by a competent person. Where safe, first aid can be provided while waiting for emergency assistance, and all workers must be accounted for at the designated assembly point.
Only trained and authorised personnel may carry out a rescue, and the safety of rescuers will always take priority.

5.3 Records

All incidents, near misses, and rescues must be recorded in the site accident book. The records should include details of the incident, actions taken, and any follow-up measures to prevent recurrence. Regular reviews of rescue procedures will be conducted to ensure their effectiveness and update them as necessary.

Section 6: Roles & Responsibilities

6.1 Personnel On Site

We anticipate the following personnel will be utilised on site. Between --/-- operatives, trained and experienced, will be carrying out the works in the area(s) listed in Section 4. All operatives will be advised of the method in which the works are to be carried out and will attend the site induction.

6. 2 Site Supervisors

  • The Site Supervisor is responsible for direct supervision of the workforce, ensuring that work is carried out safely, efficiently, and in accordance with approved RAMS and instructions from the Site Manager. Key duties include:
  • Delivering daily briefings and toolbox talks, and ensuring operatives fully understand their tasks and associated risks.
  • Monitoring work areas to confirm that safe systems of work are followed and PPE requirements are met.
  • Checking that permits to work, service drawings, and temporary works approvals are in place before starting activities.
  • Maintaining site discipline, housekeeping, and segregation between plant, vehicles, and pedestrians.
  • Inspecting excavations, lifting operations, and access equipment, and reporting any defects or unsafe conditions immediately.
  • Ensuring emergency equipment, rescue arrangements, and first aid provisions are readily available and understood by the team.
  • Stopping work if conditions become unsafe and escalating issues to the Site Manager.

6. Site Operatives

  • Operatives are responsible for carrying out their work safely, following training, instructions, and the requirements of the site‚Äôs RAMS and permit systems. They must take reasonable care of themselves and others who may be affected by their actions. Key duties include:
  • Attending site inductions, briefings, and toolbox talks, and working in accordance with the instructions provided.
  • Reading and understanding the relevant RAMS before starting work, and asking for clarification where required.
  • Using only approved tools, plant, and equipment, and conducting pre-use checks.
  • Wearing and maintaining the correct PPE as specified for each task.
  • Reporting any unsafe conditions, defects, or near misses immediately to the Supervisor.
  • Following traffic management, exclusion zones, and service avoidance procedures at all times.
  • Maintaining good housekeeping, respecting environmental controls, and assisting in keeping the site clean and tidy.
  • Participating in emergency drills and knowing the location of first aid, fire points, and assembly areas.

Aim To Reduce the risk to low if possible.
List the hazards associated with an activity Review the task/activity and estimate using Low (L), Medium (M), High (H) the likehood of harm being caused to the person or persons in proximity. Then do the same for teh severity using the same (L,M,H) metrics.
Results
  • Combined risk = LOW: the activity does not require further attention beyond the standard 'good practice' controls included in the Construction Health and Safety Plan (or Environmental Management Plan), site rules and relevant procedures / guidance.
  • Combined risk = MEDIUM: the activity could be significant and management control is important. Manage through Method Statements and or other management plans or procedures specific to the project. Additional training or resources may be required.
  • Combined risk = HIGH: deemed to be significant and senior management control is of a high priority. Consider design changes. Best Practice measures should be adopted.
